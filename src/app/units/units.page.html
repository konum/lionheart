Units details Cavalry King The King is the most important unit of the army. If he dies, the player loses the battle.
Fearless: The unit never flees.
Move 4: The unit can move up to 4 cells with one movement. Cavalry Knight The Knights are a useful unit for flanking and charging enemy units. But beware, if you send them alone they can be quickly overrun.
Move 4: The unit can move up to 4 cells with one movement. Infantry Infantry Infantry is the backbone of your army. No special rules apply.
 Infantry Archer Archers are a very useful unit, as they can attack from a distance.
Distance attack: Archers can hit any enemy within a 3x3 field in front of them, even over other units. Infantry Heavy Infantry Heavy infantry is the deadliest unit in combat, but are slow to move. Is the only unit that can attack twice in the same turn.
Slow: Movement cost 2 action points.
Poleaxe: This unit can attack in every direction, even backwards. Infantry Mercenary Mercenaries are a very special unit. They can cause other units to retreat and even cause panic in the enemy army, but can be bribed by the enemy king.
Fearless: This unit never flees.
Scary: For each Panic rolled in an attack, the enemy unit retreats 1 square. If the enemy unit encounters a friendly unit, panic will spread to it.
Mercenary: If the mercenaries are besides the enemy king, they will switch to the enemy army. This can be prevented having your own king besides your mercenaries. Infantry Peasants Peasants are common folk fighting for your king. They are very unpredictible: they will hit with anything, but can panic very easily. For example if they roll 2 axes, 1 arrow and 1 panic they will get 2 hits and panic 1 square.
Coward: For each Panic rolled in an attack, this unit will panic 1 square. Panic can spread to other units in the way. Hits are also resolved.
Easy hit: This unit will hit with axes or arrows, whatever rolls more, but not both.
